About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in mathematics at the Department of Mathematics, University of Florence, under the supervision of Caterina Stoppato. My research focuses on hypercomplex analysis, with particular emphasis on Dunkl operators and quaternionic analysis.

About Me

Welcome! I am Giulio Binosi, a researcher specializing in hypercomplex analysis, harmonic analysis, slice analysis, Dunkl analysis and complex and differential geometry. I work primarily with quaternionic and Clifford algebras. I completed my PhD at Trento University, now I am a Postdoc at Florence University with INdAM's project SUNRISE.

Research Interests

  • Hypercomplex Analysis
  • Harmonic Analysis
  • Slice Analysis
  • Dunkl Analysis
  • Complex Geometry
  • Differential Geometry

Publications

  1. Binosi, G. (2025). "Polyharmonicity, Almansi-type decompositions and Fueter-Sce theorem for several Clifford variables" , Bollettino dell’Unione Matematica Italiana.
  2. Binosi, G., De Bie, H., Lian, P. (2025). "Dunkl approach to slice regular functions" , Annali di Matematica Pura ed Applicata.
  3. Binosi, G. (2025). "Slice regular holomorphic Cliffordian functions of order k" , Adv. Appl. Clifford Algebras.
  4. Binosi, G. (2024). "Almansi-Type Decomposition for Slice Regular Functions of Several Quaternionic Variables" , Complex Anal. Oper. Theory.
  5. Binosi, G. (2023). "Partial slice regularity and Fueter's theorem in several quaternionic variables" , Complex Manifolds.

Teaching
